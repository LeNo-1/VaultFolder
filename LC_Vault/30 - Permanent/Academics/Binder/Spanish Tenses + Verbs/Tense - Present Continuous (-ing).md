> [!Note] 
> The present participle in Spanish, known as "el gerundio," is used to describe an ongoing action. It is equivalent to the "-ing" form in English (e.g., "walking," "eating," "living").
 
## Formation of Regular Present Participles

The formation of regular present participles is straightforward and depends on the ending of the infinitive verb.

### 1. -AR Verbs

| Infinitive | Meaning  | Present Participle | Meaning  |
| :--------- | :------- | :----------------- | :------- |
| hablar     | to speak | hablando           | speaking |
| caminar    | to walk  | caminando          | walking  |
| estudiar   | to study | estudiando         | studying |
| cantar     | to sing  | cantando           | singing  |

**Examples:**
*   Ella está **hablando** por teléfono. (She is **talking** on the phone.)
*   Nosotros estamos **caminando** en el parque. (We are **walking** in the park.)

### 2. -ER Verbs

| Infinitive | Meaning  | Present Participle | Meaning  |                                          |
| :--------- | :------- | :----------------- | :------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| comer      | to eat   | comiendo           | eating   |                                          |
| aprender   | to learn | aprendiendo        | learning |                                          |
| correr     | to run   | corriendo          | running  |                                          |
| leer       | to read  | leyendo            | reading  | *(See note on irregular 'y' form below)* |

**Examples:**
*   Estoy **comiendo** una manzana. (I am **eating** an apple.)
*   Ustedes están **aprendiendo** español. (You all are **learning** Spanish.)

### 3. -IR Verbs

| Infinitive | Meaning  | Present Participle | Meaning  |                                             |
| :--------- | :------- | :----------------- | :------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| vivir      | to live  | viviendo           | living   |                                             |
| escribir   | to write | escribiendo        | writing  |                                             |
| abrir      | to open  | abriendo           | opening  |                                             |
| dormir     | to sleep | durmiendo          | sleeping | *(See note on irregular stem change below)* |

**Examples:**
*   Ellos están **viviendo** en Madrid. (They are **living** in Madrid.)
*   Yo estoy **escribiendo** un correo. (I am **writing** an email.)

## Irregular Present Participles

### 1. Stem-Changing -ER and -IR Verbs

Many stem-changing verbs (e.g., `e` > `ie`, `o` > `ue`, `e` > `i`) maintain a stem change in the present participle.

*   **e > i change:** This occurs in many -IR verbs where the 'e' in the stem changes to an 'i'.
    *   `pedir` (to ask for) -> **pidiendo** (asking for)
    *   `sentir` (to feel) -> **sintiendo** (feeling)
    *   `servir` (to serve) -> **sirviendo** (serving)
    *   `decir` (to say) -> **diciendo** (saying)
    *   `venir` (to come) -> **viniendo** (coming)

*   **o > u change:** This occurs in some -IR verbs where the 'o' in the stem changes to a 'u'.
    *   `dormir` (to sleep) -> **durmiendo** (sleeping)
    *   `morir` (to die) -> **muriendo** (dying)

### 2. Verbs Ending in a Vowel + -ER or -IR

If the stem of an -ER or -IR verb ends in a vowel, the `-iendo` ending changes to **-YENDO** to avoid having three vowels in a row.

| Infinitive | Meaning | Present Participle | Meaning |
| :--------- | :------ | :----------------- | :------ |
| leer       | to read | leyendo            | reading |
| caer       | to fall | cayendo            | falling |
| oír        | to hear | oyendo             | hearing |
| construir  | to build | construyendo       | building |
| huir       | to flee | huyendo            | fleeing |

**Examples:**
*   Están **leyendo** un libro interesante. (They are **reading** an interesting book.)
*   El niño está **cayendo**. (The child is **falling**.)

### 3. Other Irregular Forms

*   `ir` (to go) -> **yendo** (going)

## Usage of the Present Participle

The most common use of the present participle in Spanish is to form progressive tenses with the verb `estar` (to be).

**Formula:** `ESTAR` (conjugated) + `PRESENT PARTICIPLE`

**Examples:**
*   Yo **estoy trabajando**. (I **am working**.)
*   Tú **estás bailando**. (You **are dancing**.)
*   Nosotros **estamos aprendiendo**. (We **are learning**.)
*   Ellos **están saliendo**. (They **are leaving**.)
