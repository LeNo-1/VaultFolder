## Present Reflexive

> [!Note] A verb is **reflexive** when the *subject and object* of the verb is the *same*

**Example:**

| I wash the car | Yo lavo el coche |
| -------------- | ---------------- |
| I wash myself  | Yo me lavo       |

### Actions related to personal care and habitual routines:

| ----------- | ---------------------------- | ---------------- |

| cepillarese | to brush                     | me cepillo<br>   |

| lavarse     | to wash oneself              | me lavo          |
| levantarse  | to get up/stand up           | me levanto       |

### Verbs that change their meaning when in reflexive

| acordar  | to agree          | acordarse           | to remember    |
| -------- | ----------------- | ------------------- | -------------- |
| llamar   | to call           | llamarse            | to be called   |
| llevar   | to take/weaer     | llevarse bien (con) | to get on with |
| negar    | to deny           | negarse             | to refuse      |
| parecer  | to appear         | parecerse (a)       | to look like   |
| probar   | to try/test/taste | probarse            | to try on      |
| quitar   | to take away      | quitarse            | to take off    |
| volver   | to return         | vovlerse            | to become      |
| preparar | to prepare        | prepararse          | to get ready   |
### Reflexive verb can be used to intensify the action

| ir       | to go          | comer   | to eat                   |
| -------- | -------------- | ------- | ------------------------ |
| irse     | to go away     | comerse | to eat up (to gobble up) |
| dormir   | to sleep       | caer    | to fall                  |
| dormirse | to fall asleep | caerse  | to fall down/off         |
